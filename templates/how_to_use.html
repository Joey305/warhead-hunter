Workflow Guide

How to Use Warhead Hunter

Warhead Hunter helps researchers convert ligand-bound protein structures into atom-level solvent exposure maps. A typical workflow begins with a target name, a structure-search query, and an optional FASTA sequence. The platform retrieves candidate structures, filters ligand-containing complexes, computes ligand atom SASA, and presents the results in synchronized 2D and 3D views.

Launch Warhead Hunter Science Docs

Step 1: Choose a Target

Enter a target name such as KRAS, BTK, EGFR, BRD4, or another protein of interest.

Step 2: Define a Structure Search

Use a search phrase that captures the target and ligand-bound structures you want to inspect.

Step 3: Add FASTA Sequence When Available

A FASTA sequence can help guide chain selection and filtering when the search term is broad or ambiguous.

Step 4: Launch the Scan

Warhead Hunter prepares the structures, computes ligand exposure, and assembles browser-ready result files.

Step 5: Inspect Candidate Atoms

Use the results gallery to compare ligand summary cards, 2D exposure maps, and 3D binding-site views.

Interpretation Note

Warhead Hunter identifies solvent-exposed ligand atoms for inspection. It does not guarantee that a highlighted atom is synthetically accessible, potency-preserving, or optimal for a specific degrader or covalent inhibitor design.
